We offer the expertise of four physical therapists (licensed through the state of California) and six support staff. Valerie Nelson Physical Therapy has over 60 years of combined clinical experience. With every patient we see, we are committed to:

Excellence—We are committed to constant improvement and continuing education.

Continuity of Care—Our patients see the same therapist from their first assessment to completion of rehabilitation.

Communication—Maintaining contact with the patient’s physician, case manager and insurance company is a priority to us.

Convenience—We have extended hours designed for a variety of work and school schedules.

Community—All our staff live and work in Oroville and support the community by participating and volunteering in school/sports/church activities. Our office also aids local programs in training students in physical therapy.

At Valerie Nelson Physical Therapy, we focus on six approaches to treatment:

Manual Therapy–"hands on" joint and soft tissue mobilization, instrument-assisted soft tissue mobilization

Guided Exercise–strengthening, stretching, posture re-education, endurance and balance.

Integrating Function–movement retraining for daily activities.

Therapeutic Modalities–pain relieving modalities such as electrical stimulation, ultrasound, iontophoresis, cervical/lumbar traction, heat/ice.

Kinesio-taping–specialized taping technique to support tissue structures, relieve pain, reduce swelling.

Education–understanding the cause of the problem and the tools to facilitate rehabilitation.

We offer a wide spectrum of rehabilitative services: General Musculoskeletal Rehabilitation: Rehabilitation for impairments ranging from neck and low back pain, ankle approaches to treatment

Sports Medicine Rehabilitating injuries, from competitive sports to the “weekend athlete.”

Pre-or Post-Surgical Rehabilitation before or after a surgery, which may include re-education, endurance and balance.

Pelvic Floor Dysfunction Rehabilitating the weak pelvic floor muscles that cause urinary or bowel urgency, leakage or bedwetting. These issues may be due to postpartum, pre- or post-menopause symptoms, hip, colon or prostate surgeries. We utilize the “roll for control” program, which focuses on specific exercises and education regarding foods/relaxation/biofeedback techniques.
